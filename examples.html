ESC in practice

How the 5-part methodology helps each committee

These are potential examples, not mandates. They show how ESC can help VNC ICU committees turn ideas and concerns into clearer, safer, committee-owned action.

Why tier the risk?

Risk tiering turns “Can we do this?” into “What support does this need?”

For nurses, risk tiering is not bureaucracy. It protects bedside judgment, patient safety, staff safety, and committee momentum. It helps a committee know whether an idea can move forward locally, needs UBC discussion, or must be escalated before action.

Green · Local action

Low-risk work the committee can usually move forward with: reminders, education drafts, recognition ideas, simple templates, or workflow clarification that does not change clinical practice or staffing coverage.

Nurse value: keeps small improvements from getting stuck.

Yellow · Discuss and coordinate

Work that may affect workflow, documentation, supplies, scheduling, education validation, data, budget, or more than one committee. It needs the right voices before action.

Nurse value: prevents rework and brings the right people in early.

Red · Escalate before action

Anything touching patient harm risk, staff safety, scope of practice, policy, PHI, regulatory concerns, serious events, or clinical decision support. These need leadership, quality/safety, or appropriate expert review.

Nurse value: protects patients, nurses, and the unit from unintended harm.

Plain-language rule: Green means “we can probably move.” Yellow means “pause and bring the right people in.” Red means “do not move alone.”

Current committees and projects

Examples by committee/project

Each example follows the same ESC rhythm: consult first, tier the risk, route expertise, close the loop, and standardize what works.

CLABSI

Consult first
Clarify whether the focus is line necessity, dressing integrity, tubing practices, CHG documentation, audit follow-up, or education needs.
Tier the risk
Green: quick reminder or checklist update. Yellow: repeated documentation or maintenance gaps. Red: active rise in CLABSI events requiring infection prevention, quality, and leadership escalation.
Route expertise
Connect infection prevention, vascular access, CNS/educator, charge nurses, and bedside champions.
Close the loop
Track audits, barriers, intervention completion, and whether the trend improves.
Standardize what works
Convert successful steps into a CLABSI rounding checklist or line-care bundle quick guide.

HAPI

Consult first
Clarify whether help is needed with turn compliance, device-related injury prevention, staging awareness, supplies, or staff education.
Tier the risk
Green: education reminder. Yellow: recurring offloading or documentation gaps. Red: increase in HAPI events or serious breakdown requiring urgent interdisciplinary review.
Route expertise
Bring in wound care, PT/OT, CNS/educator, supply/resource nurses, charge nurses, and bedside staff caring for high-risk patients.
Close the loop
Track intervention completion, audit findings, barrier themes, and monthly trend updates.
Standardize what works
Build a prevention toolkit: turn prompts, device offloading guide, skin huddle template, and high-risk rounding script.

Safety

Consult first
Define the safety issue: falls, medication safety, alarm fatigue, workplace violence, equipment issues, line/tube dislodgement, or escalation delays.
Tier the risk
Green: workflow clarification. Yellow: recurring near-miss pattern. Red: patient harm, staff injury risk, sentinel-level concern, or urgent escalation need.
Route expertise
Connect charge nurses, pharmacy, respiratory therapy, security, quality/safety, unit leadership, and frontline nurses.
Close the loop
Track reports, near-miss themes, staff feedback, and whether fixes reduce recurrence.
Standardize what works
Create a safety intake form, escalation pathway, “what to do when...” guide, or monthly safety snapshot for UBC.

Rest and Recreation

Consult first
Clarify whether the aim is morale, decompression, team connection, recognition, break culture, or burnout mitigation.
Tier the risk
Green: low-cost staff engagement activity. Yellow: event needing schedule, budget, or manager approval. Red: anything affecting safe staffing, patient coverage, or fairness across shifts.
Route expertise
Include charge nurses, staffing/scheduling leads, leadership, night-shift representatives, and wellness resources.
Close the loop
Track participation, staff feedback, shift equity, and whether activities benefit the whole team.
Standardize what works
Create an event planning template, recognition calendar, micro-rest menu, or day/night shift inclusion checklist.

Professional Development

Consult first
Clarify the need: certification, preceptor growth, clinical ladder, journal club, skills refreshers, conference sharing, or leadership development.
Tier the risk
Green: topic sharing or resource list. Yellow: training requiring coverage or competency validation. Red: education tied to high-risk practice change or policy-sensitive care.
Route expertise
Connect educators, CNS, certified nurses, leadership, clinical ladder mentors, and unit subject-matter experts.
Close the loop
Track attendance, certification interest, completed applications, skill validation, and staff-reported confidence.
Standardize what works
Create a development roadmap, certification study group template, clinical ladder checklist, or UBC presentation guide.

Mentorship

Consult first
Define the mentorship model: new-to-ICU, new-to-unit, charge nurse growth, certification support, or leadership mentorship.
Tier the risk
Green: informal mentor matching. Yellow: structured mentorship with expectations, time commitments, or sensitive feedback. Red: unsafe practice, bullying, impairment, or performance issues requiring leadership escalation.
Route expertise
Connect mentors with preceptors, educators, charge nurses, experienced ICU nurses, leadership, and professional development partners.
Close the loop
Track matches, check-in completion, common barriers, retention themes, and perceived support.
Standardize what works
Build a mentor-match form, first-meeting guide, monthly check-in template, and escalation pathway for concerns outside mentorship scope.

Pathways to Excellence

Consult first
Clarify what evidence, stories, workflows, or staff engagement data the Pathways work needs from ICU committees.
Tier the risk
Green: collect examples and narratives. Yellow: evidence gaps, unclear ownership, or committee participation gaps. Red: missing required elements or timeline risks affecting readiness.
Route expertise
Connect UBC chairs, shared governance leads, nursing leadership, educators, quality partners, and frontline staff storytellers.
Close the loop
Track evidence requests, owners, due dates, missing documents, and submitted materials.
Standardize what works
Create an evidence tracker, story capture template, committee contribution checklist, and ICU examples repository.

Beacon Award

Registration has been submitted.

Consult first
Identify what is needed after registration: evidence collection, gap analysis, timeline planning, writing support, staff engagement, or outcome data organization.
Tier the risk
Green: gather existing examples. Yellow: missing data, unclear ownership, or timeline pressure. Red: critical evidence gaps, outcome concerns, or submission deadlines at risk.
Route expertise
Connect quality, leadership, educators, CNS, bedside champions, UBC committees, and data/reporting partners.
Close the loop
Maintain a progress dashboard by standard, evidence owner, due date, draft status, and unresolved gap.
Standardize what works
Build a Beacon evidence binder, narrative template, data request tracker, and committee evidence contribution guide.

Sutter Health Innovation Challenge 2026

For ICU member submissions.

Consult first
Help ICU members shape ideas into clear problem statements: pain point, affected users, desired outcome, and support needed.
Tier the risk
Green: workflow improvement or education idea. Yellow: technology, data, staffing workflow, or interdepartmental coordination. Red: patient safety, PHI, clinical decision support, regulatory concerns, or AI use requiring review.
Route expertise
Connect submitters with nurse innovators, informatics, quality/safety, educators, leadership, finance/operations, and AI/data governance reviewers when relevant.
Close the loop
Track submitters, idea stage, deadlines, feedback received, and next actions before final submission.
Standardize what works
Create an innovation submission template: problem, current state, proposed solution, expected impact, risk tier, stakeholders, metrics, and pitch summary.

Shared infrastructure

What ESC can build once and reuse across committees

The same tools can support CLABSI, HAPI, Safety, Rest and Recreation, Professional Development, Mentorship, Pathways, Beacon, and Innovation Challenge work.

Committee intake form

Captures what each committee needs help with.

Green / Yellow / Red tier

Clarifies what can move locally, what needs coordination, and what must escalate.

Action log

Tracks owner, next step, due date, barrier, and UBC report-back.

Committee dashboard

Shows active work without adding meeting burden.

Monthly UBC summary

Gives a clean view of progress, barriers, and wins.

Template library

Prevents every committee from reinventing agendas, trackers, and report-outs.

One rhythm for many kinds of work.

ESC helps committees move from idea → clarified aim → risk-aware plan → right expertise → action → visible follow-up → reusable practice.
