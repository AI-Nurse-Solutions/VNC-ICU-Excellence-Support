Who we are

A facilitation hub for VNC ICU shared governance

The VNC ICU Unit-Based Council works through committees that improve practice, operations, quality, education, engagement, and the work environment. The Excellence Support Committee does not replace those committees. We make their work easier to start, safer to evaluate, and simpler to carry through.

Mission

To serve as the backbone of shared governance in the VNC ICU by equipping, guiding, and elevating every UBC committee. We provide facilitation, decision support, templates, and follow-through so committees can turn ideas into safe, visible, committee-owned action.

Vision

A VNC ICU where every UBC committee leads with confidence, collaborates across boundaries, and can show progress on the work it owns. Excellence becomes a shared governance habit: clear aims, safe decisions, visible outcomes, and nurses supported to lead change from the bedside outward.

Our role

Facilitate, organize, connect, document, and help remove barriers for VNC ICU UBC committees.

Not our role

We do not take over committee decisions, bypass the UBC, or approve clinical changes outside the proper chain.

How success shows up

Each committee leaves with a clear aim, an owner, a risk tier, next steps, and a simple way to report progress back to UBC.

How we help

Our working methodology

Every engagement follows the same practical rhythm: listen, clarify risk, connect expertise, track progress, and transfer the tools back to the committee. The committee stays in the driver's seat; ESC provides the structure.

01

Consult first

Start with the committee's real issue, current workflow, constraints, and desired outcome before offering a solution.

02

Tier the risk

Use Green / Yellow / Red guardrails to decide what can move locally, what needs UBC discussion, and what must escalate.

03

Route expertise

Bring in the right clinical, operational, educator, quality, or leadership voice while keeping committee ownership intact.

04

Close the loop

Use brief check-ins, visible action logs, and outcome notes so UBC can see what changed and what still needs help.

05

Standardize what works

Turn repeatable practices into templates, dashboards, and playbooks that other VNC ICU committees can reuse.
