Step 2

Practice the Support Method

ESC support should feel consistent from committee to committee. Step 2 practices the core method: route the right expertise, facilitate cross-committee problem solving, and close the loop with simple feedback and progress tracking.

Part 1 · Credential-Aware Support

Route the question to the right expertise

ESC helps committees identify whose voice is needed for a decision: bedside nurses, charge nurses, educators, quality/safety partners, operations leaders, or other stakeholders. The goal is better input, not transferred ownership.

  • Clarify what kind of expertise the question requires.
  • Invite the right voice at the right point in the work.
  • Return the decision and action plan to the committee that owns it.

Part 2 · Cross-Committee Collaboration

Use a repeatable structure for shared problems

Many UBC issues touch more than one committee. ESC provides a neutral facilitation structure so teams can define the problem, surface constraints, build options, and agree on next steps without losing ownership.

A working-together session, in four beats: frame the shared challenge, surface each committee's constraints, build realistic options, and assign committee-owned next steps for UBC follow-up.

Part 3 · Feedback and Iteration

Track progress without creating busywork

ESC helps committees use small, steady feedback loops: what was tried, what changed, what barrier remains, and what needs UBC support. The rhythm should clarify the work, not add another burden.

  • Choose one or two meaningful signs of progress.
  • Use brief check-ins instead of heavy retrospective meetings.
  • Bring lessons, barriers, and wins back into the next UBC cycle.
