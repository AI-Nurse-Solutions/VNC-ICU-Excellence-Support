Step 3

Build the Operating Toolkit

ESC becomes useful when support is easy to repeat. Step 3 turns the methodology into working tools: templates, facilitation routines, action logs, and check-in rhythms that help VNC ICU UBC committees sustain progress.

Part 1 · Guidance Templates

Use standard prompts for consistent committee work

Templates give every committee the same practical starting point while leaving the answers and decisions with the team. They should make the work clearer, faster, and easier to report back to UBC.

  • Capture aim, owner, stakeholder, risk tier, next step, and due date.
  • Adapt the template to the committee's actual workflow.
  • Use the completed template as the UBC update, not a separate document.

Part 2 · Collaborative Planning Sessions

Facilitate the process while committees own the decision

ESC can help set up and run planning sessions so committees spend less time organizing the meeting and more time making progress on the work they own.

What ESC handles: agenda, timekeeping, clarifying questions, decision notes, risk-tier prompt, and the action log — while the committee decides what to do next.

Part 3 · Continuous Empowerment

Support should build committee independence

ESC stays available, but the goal is not dependency. As committees gain clarity and rhythm, support shifts from hands-on facilitation to light-touch check-ins and reusable playbooks.

Standing check-ins Shared playbooks Action logs Growing autonomy

You've made it through all three steps. ESC now has a clearer role, a repeatable support method, and a practical toolkit for helping VNC ICU UBC committees move work forward while preserving committee ownership.
